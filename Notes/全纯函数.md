>[!Note] Definition 1
>Let $f:\mathbb{C}\rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ be a complex function. We say $f$ is differentiable at $\zeta_{0}$ if
>$$\lim_{ \delta \zeta \to 0 } \frac{f(\zeta_{0}+\delta \zeta)-f(\zeta_{0})}{\delta \zeta}$$
>exists and independent of $\delta \zeta$

我们可以得到：

>[!Success] Proposition 1 (Cauchy-Riemann condition)
>Let $f=\phi+i\psi$, where $\phi(\zeta),\psi(\zeta)\in \mathbb{R}$. Then if $f$ is differentiable at $\zeta_{0}$, we must have:
>$$\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}= \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial y},\ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}= - \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial x}\text{ at }\zeta_{0}$$
## Proof.

取$\delta \zeta=\delta x+i\delta y$。则：
$$\begin{align}
\frac{f(\zeta_{0}+\delta \zeta)-f(\zeta_{0})}{\delta \zeta } & = \frac{\phi(x_{0}+\delta x,y_{0}+\delta y)-\phi(x_{0},y_{0})+i\psi(x_{0}+\delta x,y_{0}+\delta y)-i\psi(x_{0},y_{0})}{\delta x+i\delta y} \\
 & = \frac{\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}\delta x+ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}\delta y+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial x}\delta x+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial y}\delta y}{\delta x+i\delta y} \\
 & = \frac{\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}+ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x}+ i\frac{\partial \psi}{\partial x}+i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial y} \frac{\delta y}{\delta x}}{1+ i \frac{\delta y}{\delta x}} \\
 & = \frac{\left( \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial x} \right)+\left( \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial y} \right) \frac{\delta y}{\delta x}}{1+i \frac{\delta y }{\delta x}}
\end{align}$$
该值需要独立于$\frac{\delta y}{\delta x}$。所以分子必定正比于分母。得到：
$$\begin{align}
 & \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial y}= i\left( \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}+i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial x} \right) \\
\implies & \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}=- \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial x},\ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}= \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial y}  
\end{align}$$
>[!Right]
>$\blacksquare$

我们还可以得到极坐标系中的Cauchy-Riemann条件：

>[!Success] Proposition 2 (Riemann-Cauchy condition)
>Let $f=\phi+i\psi$, where $\phi(\zeta),\psi(\zeta)\in \mathbb{R}$. Then if $f$ is differentiable at $\zeta_{0}$, we must have:
>$$\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}= \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial y},\ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial y}= - \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial x}\text{ at }\zeta_{0}$$
## Proof.

取$\delta \zeta= e^{i\theta }dr+ie^{i\theta}r  d\theta$。我们有：
$$\begin{align}
\frac{f(\zeta_{0}+\delta \zeta)-f(\zeta_{0})}{\delta \zeta} & = \frac{\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial r}\delta r+ \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial \theta}\delta \theta+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial r}\delta r+i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial \theta}d\theta}{e^{i\theta}\delta r+ ie^{i\theta}r  \delta \theta} \\
 & = \frac{\left(\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial r}+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial r}\right)+\left( \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial \theta}+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial \theta} \right) \frac{\delta \theta }{\delta r}}{e^{i\theta}+ ie^{i\theta}r  \frac{\delta \theta}{\delta r} }
\end{align}$$
同理：
$$\begin{align}
 &  \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial \theta}+ i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial \theta}= ir\left( \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial r}+i \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial r} \right) \\
\implies &  \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial \theta}=- r\frac{\partial \psi }{\partial r },\ r \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial r}=\frac{\partial \psi}{\partial \theta} 
\end{align}$$
>[!Right]
>$\blacksquare$


>[!Note] Definition 2
>If $f$ is differentiable over $U$ an open set, we say that $f$ is holomorphic on $U$.
## Ex:

$f(\zeta)=\mathrm{Re}(\zeta)$不全纯。因为$f(\zeta)=x$。不存在：
$$\frac{\partial x}{\partial x}= \frac{\partial{0}}{\partial y}$$
