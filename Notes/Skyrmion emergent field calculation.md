Assume a skyrmion tube:
$$\begin{align}
 & \mathbf{n}=(\sin \Theta \cos \Phi,\sin \Theta \sin \Phi,\cos \Theta) \\
 & \Theta=\Theta(r)=2 \arctan\left(  \frac{R}{r} \right),\ \Phi=\Phi(\phi)=\phi+ \frac{\pi}{2}
\end{align}$$
Know:
$$\Omega_{\alpha}=\frac{1}{2}\epsilon^{\alpha \beta \gamma}\mathbf{n}\cdot(\partial_{\beta}\mathbf{n}\times \partial_{\gamma}\mathbf{n})\text{ in Cartesian coordinates}$$
Then:
$$\Omega_{z}= \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{n}\cdot\left( \partial_{r}\mathbf{n}\times \frac{1}{r}\partial_{\phi}\mathbf{n} \right)\implies \Omega_{z}=  \frac{-R}{r(r^{2}+R^{2})}\sin \Theta$$
$$\begin{align}
 & \Omega_{r}= \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{n}\cdot\left(  \frac{1}{r}\partial_{\phi}\mathbf{n}\times \partial_{z}\mathbf{n} \right)=0 \\
 & \Omega_{\phi}= \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{n}\cdot(\partial_{z}\mathbf{n}\times \partial_{r}\mathbf{n})=0
\end{align}$$
