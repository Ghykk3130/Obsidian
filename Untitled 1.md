The reading examines the creation of Wang Hui’s Southern Inspection Tour scrolls as a major Qing historiographic project. The author argues that the commission was not mere documentation but a calculated political act, blending art and imperial propaganda to legitimize Kangxi’s rule. By synthesizing classical landscape traditions with precise narrative detail, Wang Hui presented the emperor as a Confucian sage and unifier, visually integrating the southern literati into the Qing order. The scrolls thus function both as monumental art and as sophisticated instruments of political persuasion, reshaping court style while advancing Kangxi’s cultural and ideological agenda.
