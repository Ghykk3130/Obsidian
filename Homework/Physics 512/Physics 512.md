# Sakurai 5.30
## (a)
We have equations:
$$\begin{align}
 &  i\hbar   \dot{c}_{1}= V_{12} e^{i\omega_{12}t}c_{2},\ i\hbar  \dot{c}_{2}= V_{21} e^{i\omega_{21}t}c_{1} \\
\implies &    \dot{c}_{1}= \frac{\gamma}{i\hbar}e^{i(\omega_{12}+\omega)t}c_{2},\  \dot{c}_{2}= \frac{\gamma}{i\hbar}e^{i(\omega_{21}-\omega)t}c_{1}
\end{align}$$
We take derivatives to decouple the equations:
$$\begin{align}
 &   \ddot{c}_{1}= - \frac{\gamma^{2}}{\hbar^{2}}c_{1},\ \ddot{c}_{2}= - \frac{\gamma^{2}}{\hbar^{2}}c_{2}
 \\
\implies & c_{1}=c_{1}(0)\exp\left(  i \frac{\gamma}{\hbar}t \right) = \exp\left( i \frac{\gamma}{\hbar}t \right),\ c_{2}=c_{2}(0)\exp\left( i \frac{\gamma}{\hbar}t \right) =0\end{align}$$
Then $|c_{1}(t)|^{2}=1,\ |c_{2}(t)|^{2}=0$.
