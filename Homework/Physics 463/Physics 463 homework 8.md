# Kittel 8.1
## (a)
We have:
$$E_{d}= \frac{13.6}{\epsilon^{2}} \frac{m_{e}}{m}\text{ eV}\approx 629.63\ \mu\text{eV}$$
## (b)
We have:
$$a_{d}= \frac{0.53\epsilon^{}}{m_{e} /m} \mathring{A}=636\ \mathring{A}=63.6\text{ nm}$$
## (c)
Assume that the concentration is such that there is one impurity ion per $a_{d}^{3}$. Then the concentration is given by:
$$n_{d}=\frac{1}{a_{d}^{3}}\approx 3.9\times 10^{21}\ m^{-3}$$
If we use Mott criterion, then we have:
$$\begin{align}
 & n_{d}^{1/3}a_{d}= 0.25 \\
 & n_{d}\approx 6\times 10^{19}\ m^{-3}
\end{align}$$
So the concentration should roughly be between $10^{20}$ and $10^{21}$.
# Kittel 8.2
## (a)
By Kittel's book, we have:
$$n=(n_{0}N_{d})^{1/2} \exp(- E_{d} / 2kT),\ n_{0}=2\left( \frac{m_{e}kT}{2\pi \hbar^{2}} \right)^{3/2}$$
We have:
$$\begin{align}
n_{0} & \approx 2\left[  \frac{0.01\times 9\times{1}0^{-31}\times{1}.38\times{1}0^{-23}\times{4}}{2\pi \times(1.05\times{1}0^{-34})^{2}} \right]^{3/2}\approx 3.8\times 10^{19}\ m^{-3}
\end{align}$$
Then:
$$\begin{align}
n\approx (3.8\times 10^{19}\times 10^{13} \times 10^{6})^{1/2}\exp\left( - \frac{1\times 10^{-3}\times 1.6 \times 10^{-19}}{2\times 1.38 \times 10^{-23}\times 4} \right)\approx 4.5 \times 10^{18}\ m^{-3}
\end{align}$$
## (b)
We know that the Hall resistivity is given by $\rho_{yx}= \frac{B}{ne}$. Then the Hall coefficient is:
$$\begin{align}
R_{H} & = \frac{\partial \rho_{yx}}{\partial B}  \\
 & = \frac{1}{ne} \\
 & \approx -1.39 \ m^{3}C^{-1}
\end{align}$$

# Kittel 9.4
## (a)
The reciprocal lattice vector lengths are:
$$b_{1}=\pi\ \mathring{A}^{-1}=10^{8}\pi\ cm^{-1},\ b_{2}=\frac{\pi}{2}\ \mathring{A}^{-1}=\frac{\pi}{2}\times 10^{8}\ cm^{-1}$$

## (b)

Since the valency is one per primitive cell, we know that the area of the Fermi circle is half of the area of the FBZ, due to the spin degeneracy. Therefore, if $r$ is the radius of the Fermi circle:
$$\begin{align}
 & \pi r^{2}= \frac{1}{2} b_{1}b_{2} \\
\implies & r= \sqrt{ \frac{b_{1}b_{2}}{2\pi} }= \frac{\sqrt{ \pi }}{2}\times{1}0^{8}\ cm^{-1}\approx 8.86\times 10^{7}\ cm^{-1}
\end{align}$$
